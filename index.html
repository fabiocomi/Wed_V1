Frequently Asked Questions

What's the dress code?

Semi-formal or cocktail attire. For women, this could be a cocktail dress or dressy separates. For men, a suit or sport coat with dress pants. The ceremony will be in a church, so please avoid overly casual clothing.

Are children welcome?

While we love your little ones, our wedding will be an adults-only event so everyone can relax and enjoy the evening. We appreciate you making arrangements ahead of time.

Can I take photos during the ceremony?

We're having an "unplugged" ceremony. We kindly ask that all phones and cameras be put away during the ceremony. Our professional photographer will capture this special moment, and we're happy to share the photos with you afterward. After the ceremony, feel free to take as many photos as you'd like!

What time should I arrive?

Please arrive at least 30 minutes before the ceremony start time (by 2:30 PM) to allow time for parking and seating. The ceremony will begin promptly at 3:00 PM.

Is there transportation between venues?

Yes! We'll have shuttle buses running between the ceremony and reception venues from 3:45 PM to 4:15 PM. The reception venue is about a 15-minute drive from the church. If you miss the shuttle, rideshares are readily available in the area.

Our Story

How We Met

We first crossed paths in the most ordinary of places - a crowded coffee shop on a rainy Tuesday morning. Michael was waiting in line behind Sarah, who was struggling to find her wallet while balancing an armful of books. He offered to pay for her latte, and she insisted on repaying him with a proper coffee date. The rest, as they say, is history.

The Proposal

On our 5th anniversary, Michael planned what I thought was just a special dinner at our favorite restaurant. After dessert, he suggested we take a walk along the river where we'd had our first date. When we reached the exact spot where we first kissed, he got down on one knee under the stars. Of course, I said yes through happy tears!
